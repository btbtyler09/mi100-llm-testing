## Recommendations

- Use c=2 (interactive-optimal by TTFT/TPOT p99; per-user 34.3 tok/s) for interactive UX.  
- Reserve dedicated concurrency slots for batch/inference-only workloads to prevent throughput degradation from mixed traffic patterns.  
- Cap total concurrent requests at c=4 across all endpoints, as higher concurrency sharply increases TTFT p99 without meaningful throughput gains.  
- Monitor TPOT latency growth beyond c=2; introduce adaptive queuing or request prioritization when tail latencies exceed acceptable thresholds.  
- Enable piecewise compilation only during deployment init to avoid runtime warm-up penalties affecting first-user latency.  
- Distribute load uniformly across the 4 accelerators using round-robin or weighted affinity to maintain stable per-GPU utilization and avoid straggler effects.  
- Avoid aggressive speculative decoding or large max_num_seqs unless strictly needed, as observed throughput gains diminish rapidly above baseline batching with compile+piecewise=ON.  
- Use consistent request size binning to prevent small queries from inflating TTFT due to suboptimal kernel dispatch under TRITON_ATTN.
